1800 is the 2 byte packet length. byte 20 denotes UP/DOWN position change when it is a3 we are moving up , when it is 5c we are moving down, when it is 00 we have release the associated up/down button (we stopped moving). byte 19 denotes LEFT/RIGHT position change. when it is a3, we are moving left, when it is 5c we are moving right and when it is 00 we have release the associated left/right button (we stopped moving). 
given this packet for example: 881318007ffba1c3ac9cc3c3d80fc9bfd80fc93f100000a300000000

8813 is the packet ID in little endian, follow by 1800 which is 2 bytes little endian hex payload length, followed by 7ffba1c3 (probably x position float), followed by ac9cc3c3 (probably y position float), followed by d80fc9bfd (this is definitely the player heading) , followed by 80fc93f (this is definitely the camera heading), followed by 4 bytes UNKNOWN ? 1000 which contains some positional data for sure and perhaps other data also,  followed by 00 which is the LEFT/RIGHT movement, followed by a3 which is the actual UP/DOWN movement direction, followed by 00000000 which i am not sure about.
